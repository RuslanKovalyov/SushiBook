Teriyaki

Teriyaki is a Japanese way of frying food in sweet sauce, caramelizing the sauce adds shine and texture.

The word is made up of two Japanese words - teri "shine" and yaki "fried", but in most cases the word is used to refer to the sauce itself.

Teriyaki Sauce can be cooked in advance and stored, or for one meal. the recipe for ease of conversion will be presented as 1 liter of soy sauce.This Cooking Method is an average version of popular sushi restaurants.

| cooking 10min |

INGREDIENTS

  • Soy Sauce - 100ml
  • Mirin - 100ml
    •
  • Sugar - 150gr
    •

    If you are using the optional fresh gingerroot and/or garlic, slice or chop the ginger and garlic and add it to the other ingredients.

    INSTRUCTIONS

    Mix all the ingredients in a saucepan, bring to a boil, stirring without fail! otherwise the sugar will burn and the soy sauce runs off like milk when it boils. reduce heat when bubbles appear and simmer for about 4 minutes until sauce is glossy. Remove from heat and let cool. The sauce will thicken as it cools. Pour the sauce to a sterilized jar. Store in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks.

    Another simple teriyaki sauce recipe is a 1: 1 ratio with equal parts soy sauce and white sugar. This recipe is great if you don't have Mirin, or if the ingredient isn't available to you.
